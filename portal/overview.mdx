The portal is the client-facing surface, hosted at `relay.<youragency>.com`. It
lets clients see project status and ask questions without booking a meeting —
and it never exposes your tools, only their project.

## Signing in

Clients sign in with a **magic link**: they enter their email, receive a
single-use link valid for 15 minutes, and get a 30-day portal session. Portal
sessions are signed with a secret derived from the backend's JWT secret, so a
portal token can never pass the owner guard — the two identities are separated
by signature, not just by role.

<Info>
  A sign-in request never reveals whether an email belongs to a client. You can
  also mint a link yourself from the desktop client page (**Copy sign-in link**)
  and paste it into any channel — handy before SMTP is configured.
</Info>

## What the client can and can't do

- The portal is **read + approve**. Clients don't create work there — it isn't a
  PM tool for them.
- A client sees **only their own projects**, enforced in the query layer.
- The chat answers **only** from retrieved knowledge, scoped to their projects
  in SQL. Prompt injection in ingested content can't widen that scope.

## Visibility is your call

You control what each client sees, per project, from the desktop project page —
analytics, activity feed (with or without raw code events), deliverables,
decisions, and the AI chat. A disabled section returns a 404 from the portal
API, so it's genuinely gone, not just hidden. **Approvals** are always on —
they're the core loop.

## Hosting

The portal is a static web app served at your domain, with the API paths
proxied to the backend. The [server installer](/self-hosting/server-install)
sets this up — Caddy, automatic TLS, and the portal at
`https://relay.<youragency>.com`.
