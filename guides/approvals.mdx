---
title: "Approvals"
description: "AI drafts, humans decide. The gate every client-visible action passes through."
---

Nothing AI-generated reaches a client, and no external side effect fires,
without a persisted **approval record**. Approvals are how Relay keeps a human
in the loop.

## The model

When you send a draft for approval, Relay freezes a **snapshot** of exactly
what you sent and mints a single-use, expiring **magic-link** token. The client
opens that link, sees the snapshot, and either **approves** or **requests
changes** — no account required.

<Info>
  The snapshot is frozen at send time, so what the client approves is exactly
  what they saw, even if the underlying draft changes later.
</Info>

## Where clients approve

Two paths, same result:

- **Approval page** — the magic link opens a standalone approve/request-changes
  page served by the backend. Works in any browser, no sign-in.
- **Portal inbox** — signed-in clients see a "Waiting on you" list on the
  portal's **Approvals** tab, each linking to its approval page. Decided items
  move to a history list.

## What a decision triggers

On **approve**, the downstream work runs on the transactional outbox — for a
meeting, that's syncing tasks to todos and publishing issues to the linked
repo. On **request changes**, the client's comment comes back to you and
nothing is published.

<Warning>
  A decision is final and idempotent. Re-opening or re-submitting the same
  approval link after it's decided does nothing — Relay ignores repeat
  responses rather than double-firing side effects.
</Warning>

## Approvals in the portal stay fresh

The portal polls while an approval is pending and refetches when the tab
regains focus, so an item a client just approved on the approval page moves to
their history within seconds — no manual refresh.
