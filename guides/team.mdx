title: "Team & invites"
description: "Bring your team onto your Relay with a link — they create their own accounts."
---

The first person to set up a Relay instance becomes the **owner**. Everyone
else joins by an invite link and creates their own credentials — you never
manage their passwords.

## Invite a teammate

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings → Team">
    As the owner, go to **Settings → Team**. You'll see the current members and,
    below them, invite links.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create an invite">
    Click **New invite**. Relay shows the link once — copy it then, it won't be
    shown again. Links expire after 7 days and are revocable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share it">
    Send the link to your teammate through any channel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What your teammate does

The invite link opens a branded join page with:

1. **Download the desktop app** (macOS or Linux), and
2. the link to **paste into the app**: open Relay → **"Have an invite? Join
   your agency"** → paste → pick their own name, email, and password.

On success their account is created **and the app is connected** to your server
automatically — the join hands back the backend URL, runtime URL, and token, so
there's nothing else to configure.

<Info>
  Invite tokens are stored only as hashes — a leaked database dump can't mint
  memberships. Joining teammates get the **member** role.
</Info>

## Members vs. owner

- **Members** use the full product — clients, projects, meetings, agents.
- **Only the owner** can create or revoke invites and manage the team.

<Note>
  On macOS the app is currently unsigned, so a browser-downloaded copy may be
  blocked by Gatekeeper as "damaged." The join page shows the one-time fix
  (`xattr -cr /Applications/Relay.app`). See
  [Desktop install](/self-hosting/desktop-install).
</Note>
