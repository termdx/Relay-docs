Relay has a small vocabulary. Learn these five words and every screen makes
sense.

## The vocabulary

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Workspace" icon="server">
    Your self-hosted Relay instance, managed by the runtime. One workspace per
    deployment. It holds every client, project, integration, and AI provider.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Client" icon="user">
    The agency's customer. Everything Relay tracks hangs off a client. Clients
    sign in to the portal — never the desktop.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Project" icon="folder">
    A client engagement. The project is where the timeline, todos, decisions,
    meetings, and knowledge live. A client can have many projects.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Owner" icon="key">
    You — the freelancer or agency running Relay. The owner works in the
    desktop app and has full workspace access. Team members join by invite.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Knowledge base" icon="brain">
    The per-project source of truth. Every tracked signal is embedded here, and
    every Relay AI answer is grounded in it with citations.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The two surfaces

Relay is used from two places, and they never overlap:

| Surface | Who | What |
|---|---|---|
| **Desktop app** | You and your team | The control plane — create clients, review AI drafts, manage integrations and the workspace |
| **Client portal** | Your clients | A branded web app at `relay.<youragency>.com` — status, approvals, and the Relay AI chat |

## The core loop

Every feature strengthens one loop:

```text
signal (meeting, commit, message)
      │  via integrations
      ▼
tracked event on a project timeline
      │
      ▼
knowledge base ingests → source of truth
      │
      ├──▶ AI drafts next actions → you approve → pushed to your tools
      └──▶ client portal + Relay AI answer from the knowledge base
```

Two principles hold the loop together:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="AI drafts, humans decide">
    AI never writes business data or reaches a client on its own. It produces a
    **draft** attached to an approval record. Work only lands in an external
    tool — an issue created, an email sent — after you approve it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Scoping happens in the query, never the prompt">
    A client on the portal can only ever retrieve knowledge from their own
    projects, enforced in SQL. Prompt injection in ingested content can't widen
    what a client sees, because scope is never model-controlled.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The pieces

| Piece | What it is |
|---|---|
| Desktop app | The founder control plane (Tauri + React) |
| Backend | NestJS modular monolith — the system of record (Postgres + pgvector) |
| Knowledge engine | pgvector retrieval — the source of truth for every answer |
| Client portal | Where your clients see progress and talk to Relay AI |
| Runtime + CLI | The self-hosting kernel: manifests, secrets, compose, lifecycle |

<Tip>
  The runtime is what makes Relay self-hostable: it turns declarative YAML
  manifests into a running Docker stack. You rarely touch it directly — the
  desktop and installer drive it for you. See
  [Workspaces & the runtime](/self-hosting/workspaces).
</Tip>
